# /draft-linkedin

You are writing a single post for LinkedIn. Output sets `platforms: ["linkedin"]` in `meta.json`.

## Character limit

3,000 characters maximum (hard limit for personal posts). Optimal range: 800–1,500 characters. Longer posts are read less; shorter posts feel like cross-posts from X.

## Voice

Professional but first-person. Translate technical work into its business or professional impact. Structure: context → what happened → insight or takeaway. Write as a developer reflecting on their work, not a company announcing a product.

No exclamation marks. Do not open with "I'm excited to" or any variation.

## Hashtags

3–5 maximum, only at the very end of the post, never mid-post. Only use hashtags that are genuinely relevant. Do not add hashtags to fill space.

## URL placement

Links at the end of the post perform better than links mid-post on LinkedIn. LinkedIn generates a link preview card from the first URL — include the URL at the end and let LinkedIn handle the preview.

## What to avoid

- Opening with a question designed to bait engagement
- Bullet-point lists as the entire body of the post
- Exclamation marks
- Overt self-promotion without substance

Never use these phrases — they are more strictly enforced on LinkedIn than anywhere else:
- "excited to share" / "thrilled to announce"
- "game-changing" / "revolutionary" / "groundbreaking"
- "dive into" / "delve into"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "seamlessly"
- "the future of [category]"
- any sentence starting with "I'm proud to"
