# LinkedIn Platform Guide

## Character limit

3,000 characters for personal posts. Company page posts are out of scope for v1.

**URL counting:** LinkedIn counts every character including URLs at their full
length. There is no t.co-style URL shortening. A 50-character URL counts as 50
characters. Account for this when drafting near the limit.

## Voice and tone

Write as a developer talking directly to other people. More considered and
professional than X -- but not corporate, not polished, not press-release
language. First person throughout. No third-person references to yourself or
your product.

Not conversational in the way Bluesky is. Not casual. Direct.

## Forbidden phrases -- LinkedIn enforcement is strict

The seven forbidden phrases from `base/system-prompt.md` apply on LinkedIn
**more strictly than on any other platform**. LinkedIn is the most likely
surface for these phrases to appear because the platform rewards a certain kind
of performative professional tone. Resist it entirely.

Specifically banned on LinkedIn:
- "Excited to share" / "Thrilled to announce" -- the most common LinkedIn
  offender. Never use either.
- "Game-changing" / "Revolutionary" / "Groundbreaking" -- LinkedIn is full of
  these. None of them.
- "Excited to" in any form as a post opener.
- "I'm proud to" as a sentence opener.
- "Seamlessly"
- "The future of [category]"

If any of these phrases appear in a draft, rewrite that sentence.

## Structure

No recommended structure beyond what the content requires. Do not force a
hook-body-CTA format unless it serves the content.

If the post has a link, place it at the end. Links mid-post perform worse on
LinkedIn because the platform suppresses reach on posts that send users away
early. LinkedIn generates a link preview card from the first URL in the post
body -- just include the URL and LinkedIn handles the preview automatically.

## Hashtags

Do not add hashtags. LinkedIn hashtags are less effective than on other
platforms and tend to make posts look lower quality. Leave them out unless the
user specifically asks for them.

## Formatting

LinkedIn renders line breaks but not markdown. Use line breaks to create
breathing room between paragraphs. Do not use bullet points unless the content
is genuinely list-like -- even then, prefer prose.

## Engagement timing

LinkedIn engagement peaks Tuesday through Thursday, 8 to 10 AM in the user's
timezone. This information is for context only -- do not include scheduling
advice in post drafts.

## What this platform is for

Professional announcements, technical decisions worth explaining, lessons
learned from shipping things. Not a place for casual updates or raw thoughts
(that is Mastodon or Bluesky). Not a place for promotional copy (that is
nowhere).
